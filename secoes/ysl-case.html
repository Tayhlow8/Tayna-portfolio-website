The Challenge

An experience worth stopping for

"The tool couldn't show what the idea was.
So I went to where the idea was being built."

The scroll-driven video animation — where the user's scroll position drives a frame-by-frame video playback, exactly like Apple does for hardware launches — was a first for a Brazilian beauty brand. The problem: Figma had no way to prototype this accurately. With a tight campaign deadline and no time to iterate through static handoffs, I made the call to step into the development process directly, working side-by-side with the developer until the experience felt right on every device.

The Real Work
Deadline pressure · Cross-functional delivery

When the prototype
isn't enough, you
ship alongside the dev

The centrepiece of the page was a scroll-driven video animation — as the user scrolls, the Y fragrance bottle animates frame by frame, cinematic and precise. Figma couldn't prototype this with accuracy. No static handoff would communicate what needed to happen.

With the campaign date locked and no room to iterate through rounds of misaligned feedback, I made the call to embed directly in the build. I sat with the developer through implementation — reviewing each scroll state in the browser, adjusting timing, catching edge cases on mobile — until the animation felt exactly right. The result was a fluid experience that held up on smartphones in the middle of a rock festival crowd.

Design Process

Design under pressure,
delivered with precision

01
Brief & Alignment

Aligned with YSL Beauty Brazil's marketing team on campaign goals, audience profile, and lead data requirements. Mapped the product positioning — a premium fragrance at a rock festival — as the core creative tension to design around.

02
Conceptual Direction

Defined the scroll-driven video animation as the hero interaction — a cinematic product reveal that puts the Y bottle center-stage before asking anything of the user. This reversed the typical lead-capture logic: experience first, form second.

03
Wireframing & UI Design

Delivered high-fidelity UI screens applying the campaign KV — typography, colors, and Ben Harper's visual identity. Designed the full page flow: scroll animation zone, brand storytelling, and the lead capture form with YSL's luxury aesthetic.

04
Embedded Dev Collaboration

Rather than hand off files and wait for feedback rounds, I embedded directly in the development process. Worked shoulder-to-shoulder with the developer: reviewing scroll states in the browser, adjusting easing curves, testing on real mobile devices. This was the project where I learned that the best handoff is sometimes no handoff at all — just being in the room until it's right.

Key Takeaways

What this project taught me

01
The best handoff is presence

When the prototype can't capture the intent and the deadline is real, being in the room with the developer beats any annotation or spec doc. Proximity closes gaps faster than documentation.

02
Pressure is a design constraint

A tight timeline forced a smarter process — no back-and-forth cycles, no waiting. Pressure clarified what mattered: the scroll feeling right on mobile, nothing else.

03
Design doesn't end at the file

Owning quality through implementation — not just design — is what separates a good designer from a great collaborator. The launch success came from not letting go of the work at handoff.
